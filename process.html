Recruitment Process - Doon University

Introduction

Preamble

The recruitment policies at Doon University are designed to ensure fairness, transparency, and integrity in the selection process for both students and recruiters. Our objective is to create an ecosystem that supports equal opportunities for all, enabling both companies and students to find their ideal matches.

Disclaimer

The university reserves the right to review and update recruitment policies as necessary. It is the responsibility of stakeholders to stay informed of any changes to ensure compliance with the latest guidelines.

Definitions

  • Slot: A designated time period for a company to conduct their recruitment activities.
  • Preference Order: The order of preference set by students when applying to multiple companies.
  • Extended Offer: An offer extended to a student after the initial placement phase.
  • Tests: Written or online assessments conducted by recruiters as part of their selection process.

Recruitment Process Overview

Modes of Selection

There are two main modes of selection:

  • Pre-Placement Offers (PPOs): These are offers made through internships completed by students at the company.
  • On-Campus Tests: Selection through tests and interviews conducted on-campus by companies.

Placement Process

The placement process follows a slot system where multiple companies may share the same time slot. Students are allowed a maximum of 55 applications before Phase 1 of the placement process, and spot-on offers or deferred selections may also be conducted depending on specific company policies.

Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility for participating in the recruitment process is restricted to final-year students with a minimum CPI as stipulated by the university. Students are allowed one-time participation, and international students may have specific criteria for eligibility.

For Students

Application Process

Students can apply to companies by submitting their applications through the placement portal. Preference orders must also be submitted to prioritize multiple offers.

Interview and Selection

Students are expected to conduct themselves professionally during tests and interviews. Once an offer is received, the student must decide whether to accept or decline it within a stipulated time frame.

Pre-Placement Offer (PPO) Policy

PPOs are extended to students after successful internships. If a student accepts a PPO, they are ineligible for subsequent on-campus placements.

Deferred Placement Policy

Students may opt for deferred placements if they are pursuing higher studies, entrepreneurship, or competitive exams like UPSC. The deferred placement must be formally requested and approved by the university.

Code of Conduct

All students are expected to adhere to a strict code of conduct during the recruitment process. Any misconduct may result in exclusion from the placement activities.

For Recruiters

Registration Process

Recruiters must register and submit a Job Application Form (JAF) to participate in the placement drive. They may offer multiple profiles, which should be clearly outlined in the JAF.

Recruitment Guidelines

Recruiters are expected to adhere to guidelines for conducting fair tests and interviews. The university encourages uniformity in CTC offers and clear communication regarding bonds and medical conditions.

Offer Process

Offers must be communicated to students through the placement cell, with clear timelines. Withdrawals or changes in offers are highly discouraged to maintain trust and integrity in the recruitment process.

Internship to PPO Process

Recruiters are encouraged to convert successful internships into Pre-Placement Offers (PPOs), provided the student meets the company's performance standards.

Special Considerations

Accessibility

We are committed to ensuring accessibility for all students, including those with disabilities. Specific accommodations are available upon request to ensure a fair recruitment process.

International Placements

International placements may have additional requirements or criteria, which will be communicated to eligible students in advance.

Grievance Redressal

Contact Information

For any grievances or concerns, please contact the Career Counselling Training & Placement Cell (CCTP) at placementcell@doonuniversity.ac.in.

Grievance Process

Students and recruiters may file grievances through a formal process managed by the CCTP. All complaints will be reviewed promptly to ensure a fair outcome.
